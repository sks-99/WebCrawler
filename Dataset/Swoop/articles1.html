Swoop Acquires Beam Internet, SA’s Largest Regional Fixed Wireless Provider | Naraku Atherald

danielkuhn10 hours ago

South Australian company Beam Internet has been acquired by Swoop Holdings Limited, a fixed wireless and wholesale network infrastructure provider.

Swoop announced on July 12 that it has acquired a regional wireless broadband provider in South Australia for a corporate value of $ 7.2 million.

Beam Internet is South Australia’s largest privately owned fixed wireless network, offering high-speed wireless broadband with over 60 towers and Mast’s proprietary network.

Beam Network offers over 3600 on-net housing and SME services covering the Fulreux Peninsula, Barossa, Kangaroo Island, Midnorth, Murray Bridge, Southeast South Australia, and parts of Western Victoria and Mary. It is in operation. In the area Victoria.

Beam Internet founder Josh Helbig said he was excited about the new chapter and has no plans to relocate his office from Nuriootpa in Barrosa Valley.

We look forward to seeing the network continue to grow and cover more regions of South Australia, Helwig said.

On Kangaroo Island, local technician Damon Hagerstrom will continue to be a local reseller of Beam Internet and serve the community.

The acquisition will take place within weeks of several other acquisitions, including regional wireless broadband provider Speedweb and Perth-based fixed wireless provider for residential and business, Community Communications (ComComs). ..

Swoop CEO Alex West said the acquisition of Beam is another great opportunity for Swoop to expand its infrastructure coverage.

“Beam has recently been upgraded and has a well-built, state-of-the-art network that works with our own domestic network and provides a strong foothold for growth into the SA market.” Mr West said.

We look forward to this acquisition giving us the opportunity to establish the Swoop brand’s presence in South Australia.

Bonitz Advisory is Swoop’s M & A advisor on the transaction, Maddocks is a legal advisor, and PKF is a tax advisor.

Swoop CEO, Alex West.

  • Swoop has signed an agreement to acquire 100% of South Australia-based wireless broadband provider Wan Solutions Pty Ltd (traded as Beam Internet).
  • The purchase price on a “no cash, debt-free basis” is $ 6.7 million and consists of $ 6 million in cash and $ 700,000 in Swoop shares.
  • Beam FY22EBITDA is projected to be at least $ 1.6 million.
  • Under the agreement, $ 1.3 million in cash consideration will be suspended for 12 months for potential billing or adjustments.
  • Based on the forecast EBITDA, the acquisition is expected to substantially increase revenue.
  • The cash portion of the acquisition will be funded from existing cash reserves and will be completed within four weeks.
  • Consideration shares are subject to a 12-month escrow.
  • Swoop has no bank debt and is pursuing a significant working capital and acquisition debt facility.
